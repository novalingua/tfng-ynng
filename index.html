What makes an answer False/No

When is an answer Not Given

Basis

  • False: if the statement contradicts the information
  • No: if the statement contradicts the claims of the writer

False/No answers require contradiction.

Remember, difference is NOT contradiction.

Contradiction requires opposite meaning or exclusivity.

How to determine

Negation

  • Negation is the process by which meaning is contradicted or denied.
  • Negation can occur within a word, phrase, clause, or sentence.
  • Common negations
    • Standard negation: by using not
    • Some affixes such as "un-" and "im-"
    • Certain words or phrases, like "ever", "any", "at all", etc.

Method 1

"Strong" language

"Strong" words

  • Some words can be considered "strong".
    • Comparisons (comparative, superlative, before, after, etc.)
    • Absolutes (complete, full, perfect, etc.)
    • Extremes (thorough, agree, mistaken)
  • "Strong" words often create binary logic (they can either be 1 or 0, but never both)
  • If these words are negated, the answer is probably False/No.
  • If other words in the question are negated, the answer is probably Not Given.

Examples

Other countries had built underground railways before the Metropolitan line opened.

Historians have come to an agreement about the origins of the Long Man of Wilmington.

Method 2

Adding "not"

  • Imagine adding "not" somewhere in the sentence so that the meaning is opposite.
    • Verbs are common locations to add "not"
  • Treat these locations the same as "strong" words.

Example

  • The use of ventilation shafts failed to prevent pollution in the tunnels.
    • => The use of ventilation shafts did not fail to prevent pollution in the tunnels.
  • Polar bears are able to control their levels of 'bad' cholesterol by genetic means
    • => Polar bears are not able to control their levels of 'bad' cholesterol by genetic means

Method 3

Exclusivity

  • Ask yourself "Can these things be true at the same time?"
    • It is a good idea to ensure that children come into contact with as few bacteria as possible.
    • Keeping a dog as a pet gives children early exposure to a diverse range of bacteria, which may help protect them against allergies later.
  • If they can, the answer is True/Yes or Not Given.
  • If they cannot, the answer is False/No.

Approach

  • Questions are different => no method is enough by itself.
  • Mixed approach is best: try to apply different methods.
